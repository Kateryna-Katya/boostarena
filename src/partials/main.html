Voyage

The Art of Gentle Play

The journey begins along the quieter edges of the Caribbean, where the ship waits in half-shadow. The Captain is preparing a venture he probably shouldn’t attempt — and that’s exactly why he will. Once the sails fill, the whole mood shifts. The screen should feel sun-bleached. Symbols echo the life at sea: maps curled at the edges, brass instruments, lantern glass fogged with salt spray, and those small pieces of treasure no sane sailor ignores.
This chapter isn’t a retelling of the old voyage. It’s sharper, riskier, and the stakes feel heavier.

FEATURES

Phase I
The Signal of Treasure

When the right symbols line up, boom - you're in deeper water. Nothing too crazy, just that feeling when luck finally decides to play nice.

Phase II
Captain’s Ships

These symbols broaden the playfield, revealing fragments of naval stories and tension-filled encounters beyond the usual grid.

Phase III
Walking Multiplier Wilds

Designers should visualize these not as “features,” but as restless pieces drifting across the board — each movement tightening the rope and raising the reward. No exclamation marks. Just calm, steady escalation.

Phase IV
Winds of Choice

Players steer. They choose how long to test their luck, how far to stretch the run, and when to pull back before the ocean decides otherwise.
